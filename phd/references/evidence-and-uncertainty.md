# Evidence And Uncertainty

Use this reference when claims may be uncertain, sourced, disputed, or speculative.

## Claim Types

- **Fact**: Directly supported by a reliable source or provided material.
- **Inference**: Reasoned conclusion from facts.
- **Interpretation**: A framing or explanation that may have alternatives.
- **Hypothesis**: A testable explanation or prediction.
- **Speculation**: Plausible but weakly supported idea.
- **Open question**: Important unknown that needs more evidence.

## Evidence Levels

- **A: Direct source**. The claim is directly supported by a cited or provided source.
- **B: Corroborated inference**. Multiple sources or observations support the inference.
- **C: Plausible interpretation**. The reasoning is coherent but not strongly established.
- **D: Speculative hypothesis**. Useful for exploration, not yet reliable.
- **E: Open question**. Evidence is missing or conflicting.

## Practice

For non-obvious claims:
- Mark the claim type when useful.
- Mark evidence level for important claims.
- Separate observation from interpretation.
- Name missing evidence.
- Include disconfirming evidence when available.
- Prefer hypotheses that can be tested or falsified.

## Source Notes

When using sources, record:
- title or identifier
- author or organization when known
- date when known
- URL or file path
- what the source supports
- limits of the source

## Uncertainty Language

Use direct uncertainty language:
- "This is established by..."
- "This appears to imply..."
- "A plausible interpretation is..."
- "A testable hypothesis is..."
- "The missing evidence is..."

Avoid making speculative synthesis look like sourced fact.
